Experience

I started by doing karaoke at local pubs as well as singing while playing guitar. I won a few prizes at karaoke nights and was asked to perform for a girl trying to be the next Taylor Swift as a backup singer while recording an album. I then joined Celebration Singers choir, a womens' choir that my mother was the director of. There I met a fellow member who was looking for a backup singer in their funk band, Groove Engine.

I performed with Celebration Singers and Groove Engine for a while, joined a Rock Choir, and then auditioned for the local musical theatre company, The Actor's Studio, and was cast in a few musicals, including Elf, Shrek, and The Addams Family. Unfortunately before getting to perform in those shows, I moved to London, UK to get experience as a science teacher.

“The greatest respect an artist can pay to music is to give it life.” - Pablo Casals

In London I wanted to continue singing. I was then bouncing around different choirs trying to sing as much as I could, but to also find something similar to what I had back in Canada.

I joined a gospel choir, and really enjoyed singing soul music, but didn't like the religious songs we occasionally sang. I did one performance with them, and then joined a choir that was closer to where I lived. The new choir was called West End Musical Choir and was really fun music, EXACTLY the kind I wanted to sing. I did some performances with them, and was invited to sing on their recording session at Abbey Road Studios. The choir was fun, but anyone could join, including people who didn't know much about singing so I found the songs too simplistic. They were also trying to recruit as many people as possible, so the choir performances became quite crowded.

I took a long break after that and decided to join another musical theater choir, Sing Space, and did a performance with them. However, the amount of rehearsals and not very many performance opportunities, meant that I wasn't fully in it, and decided to wait until I can find something with more performance opportunities with experienced singers!
